**Day 3: Attention and Prediction** 

**Motivation:** Answering the question: why do LLMs generate output this way? LLMs do not retrieve truth. They predict plausible continuations.

**Activity 1:** Prompt Ask an LLM to explain the same concept different ways for three different audiences:
1. A novice
2. An informed user
3. An expert

Choose a topic from within _your chosen workflow category_ that you know reasonably well. Here are some examples: 
1. Research Understanding
    - summarize a paper for a patient
    - for a graduate student
    - for a domain expert 
2. Writing
    - draft an email for a colleague
    - department chair
    - external collaborator
3. Analysis: 
    - Explain the same analysis to different audiences
    - Explain the same statistical concept (p-values, or confidence intervals) to different audience
    - Determine how to analyze a dataset for different audiences. 
4. Learning
    - explain a concept to a child
    - college student
    - expert
 
**Activity 2:** With the responses to the three audiences in hand, answer the following:
1.	What changed across the three responses? 
2.	What stayed consistent? 
3.	What assumptions about the audience did the model appear to make? 
4.	What evidence do you see that the model is predicting likely language rather than "thinking"? 
5.	How might attention mechanisms contribute to these differences? 
Include one phrase from each response in your answers to support your answers. 

**Learning goal** Participants directly observe token prediction and context dependence.

**Deliverable** 300-word reflection with excerpts.
