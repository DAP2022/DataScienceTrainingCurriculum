## Course Description

              "That is not only NOT right; it is not even wrong!" – Wolfgang Pauli

**Artificial intelligence can't replace thinking, but it can amplify poor thinking.** This one-week workshop helps participants develop the habits of mind required for fearless, evidence-based problem solving, a key skill in the age of AI. The course integrates four complementary frameworks: the Paul-Elder framework (structure of thought), Polya's heuristics (problem-solving algorithms), Wild & Pfannkuch's statistical cycle, and Kahneman's System 1/2 model, to combat cognitive offloading. Participants will not just learn the philosophy of how to think; they will diagnose why they fail.

## Course Learning Outcomes  
1.  Diagnose Cognitive Failure: Identify and articulate the root causes of error in scientific and clinical judgment, distinguishing between System 1 heuristics and System 2 analytics.
2.  Apply Frameworks of Thought: Apply the Paul-Elder Framework to decompose complex, ambiguous problems into their constituent elements and evaluate them against universal intellectual standards.
3.  Apply Statistical Intuition: Detect and correct common statistical fallacies and cognitive biases, including Base Rate Neglect, Simpson's Paradox, and P-hacking, and propose mitigation strategies.
4.  Execute Algorithmic Inquiry: Utilize Polya's 4-Step Method and the Wild & Pfannkuch Cycle to transform ad-hoc problem solving into a repeatable, rigorous investigative process.
