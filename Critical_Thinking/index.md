# Ai is a force multiplier. 
## What is the 'force'? Critical thinking! 
In the following courses, we will address:

-----------------
1. **Solving Problems** - 12 hour course. A survey of common approaches and strategies. This will include Daniel Kahneman slow and fast thinking systems, Polya's "How to Solve it" steps, Wild & Pfannkuch, and the Paul-Elder framework for general critical thinking. 
2. **Ai Literacy** - 12-hour course. How are Ai, Machine Learning, and statistics related? What is the GPT in ChatGPT? what is 'under the hood'? Can we improve our interactions with Large Language Models? How is 'Ai' used by biological researchers?
3. **Data Science Ethics** - 2-hour workshop: mo' data, mo' problems. What are the specific challenges of 'big data' that go beyond regular scientific and healthcare ethics? What are the frameworks?
4. **Data/Information Visualization** - 2-hour workshop: general guidelines to more specific principles of how our brains process visual information. Include statistical definitions (what type of variable), and Edward Tufte. 
5. **How to read Science**- a 90 minute workshop on how to read a scientific paper.

------------------
There are three pillars of Ai-augmented work: 
1. Data Biography - Data cannot 'speak for itself' and it is not neutral. This pillar of courses addresses the often opaque seemingly-small decisions about data wrangling that compound (mostly addressed in Biostatistics and Programming courses);
2. The Alignment Problem - Forces confrontation with the question: "Are we doing what we *think* that we are doing?" (mostly covered in Biostatistics and Ai Literacy course);
3. The Calibration Problem - Reasoning with uncertainty, and how to judge the output. The central question: "How confident can we be in our result?" (mostly covered in Biostatistics, Ai Literacy, and Solving Problems course).
