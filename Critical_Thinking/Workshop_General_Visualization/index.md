The slides for a 90 minute workshop given to The JAX Summer Student Program participants on July 6, 2026. 

They cover a few themes: 
1. Data Visualization matters. It is scientific reasoning made visible.
2. You must present your audience with a clear story....or they may substitute their own (and it is possible that it will be inaccurate, incomplete or unflattering!)
3. Data is NEVER NEUTRAL. Data CANNOT speak for itself.
4. Visualization intersects with Data Ethics; tell an honest story: 1. accuracy 2. artistry
5. Technical aspects of choosing which type of plot are straightforward: What type of variable and what is your question?
6. Reduce the cognitive load on your audience. The greatest value of a picture is when it forces us to notice what we never expected to see. You want **High data: ink ratio**. This is what gives your audience the most ideas in the smallest amount of space.
