# Ai is a force multiplier. What is the 'force'? Critical thinking! 

In the following courses, we will address:

-----------------
1. **Solving Problems** - a survey of common approaches and strategies. This will include Daniel Kahneman slow and fast thinking systems, Polya's "How to Solve it" steps, Wild & Pfannkuch, and the Paul-Elder framework for general critical thinking. 
2. **Ai Literacy** - How are Ai, Machine Learning, and statistics related? What is the GPT in ChatGPT? what is 'under the hood'? Can we improve our interactions with Large Language Models? How is 'Ai' used by biological researchers?
3. **Data Science Ethics** - mo' data, mo' problems. What are the specific challenges of 'big data' that go beyond regular scientific and healthcare ethics? What are the frameworks?
4. **Data/Information Visualization** - general guidelines to more specific principles of how our brains process visual information. Include statistical definitions (what type of variable), and Edward Tufte. 
5. **How to read Science**- a 90 minute workshop on how to read a scientific paper.
